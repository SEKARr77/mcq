Subject: Discrete Mathematics

Q1. In propositional logic, P AND (Q OR R) is equivalent to:
a) (P OR Q) AND (P OR R) b) (P AND Q) OR (P AND R) c) (P AND Q) AND (P AND R) d) (P OR Q) OR (P OR R)
Ans: b

Q2. A graph with no cycles is called a:
a) Complete graph b) Bipartite graph c) Tree d) Directed acyclic graph
Ans: c

Q3. The number of subsets for a set with n elements is:
a) n^2 b) 2n c) 2^n d) n!
Ans: c

Q4. If A and B are disjoint sets, then the cardinality of (A union B) is:
a) |A| * |B| b) |A| + |B| c) |A| - |B| d) 0
Ans: b

Q5. A relation which is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive is known as a(n):
a) Partial order relation b) Equivalence relation c) Empty relation d) Universal relation
Ans: b

Q6. The Pigeonhole Principle states that if k pigeons are distributed among n holes and k > n:
a) At least one hole has no pigeon b) At least one hole has exactly 2 pigeons c) At least one hole has more than one pigeon d) All holes are empty
Ans: c

Q7. Which of the following is the chromatic number of a bipartite graph with at least one edge?
a) 1 b) 2 c) 3 d) 4
Ans: b

Q8. In a simple undirected graph, the sum of degrees of all vertices is:
a) Equal to the number of edges b) Twice the number of edges c) Thrice the number of edges d) Zero
Ans: b

Q9. De Morgan's Laws state that the complement of (A union B) is:
a) A' union B' b) A' intersection B' c) A intersection B d) A' intersection B
Ans: b

Q10. A function f: A -> B is bijective if it is:
a) Injective only b) Surjective only c) Both injective and surjective d) Neither injective nor surjective
Ans: c
